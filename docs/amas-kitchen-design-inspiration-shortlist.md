# Ama's Kitchen design inspiration shortlist

Date reviewed: 2026-07-05

## Repo context checked

- Public app is a React/Vite client backed by an Express/Mongo API.
- The brand is an Accra/Lapaz delivery-first Ghanaian kitchen.
- Current public surfaces include Home, Menu, Services, About, Contact, Checkout and Order Confirmation.
- Existing design language already leans premium and warm: dark/light theme variables, accent orange, serif display headings, food photography, signature dish cards, service cards, testimonial blocks and a cart drawer.

## Shortlist

1. **Warm Accra-home palette**
   - Use palm-oil orange, cocoa brown, cream and charcoal as the core visual language.
   - Keep the current accent-led premium feel, but make the warmth feel more intentionally Ghanaian.

2. **Food photography first**
   - Prioritise close crops of jollof, waakye, grills, sides and kitchen prep.
   - Every key page should make the food feel fresh, generous and ready to order.

3. **Signature dish storytelling**
   - Keep the homepage focus on three repeat-order plates.
   - Use short decision badges such as Customer Favourite, House Special and Most Ordered.

4. **Delivery-first ordering UX**
   - Keep category pills, clear prices, quantity controls and cart drawer behaviour central.
   - Avoid decorative choices that slow the menu-to-checkout flow.

5. **Catering confidence**
   - Present events, corporate lunches and private dining as dependable food support.
   - Use clear copy around portions, custom quotes and delivery/setup expectations.

6. **Social proof and care**
   - Keep testimonials visible and pair them with contact, hours and delivery-area cues.
   - The brand should feel reachable, human and consistent.

## Review notes

- This shortlist is based on the current Ama's Kitchen repo, not generic kitchen interior trends.
- It supports the existing product direction: premium Ghanaian food, Accra delivery, quick ordering and service enquiries.
- A first implementation draft was added as `client/src/components/DesignInspirationPage.jsx`, with isolated styles and a component test.
